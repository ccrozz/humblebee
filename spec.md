## I. Executive Summary

### Mission

Humble Bee Protein Ice Cream exists to fill a glaring gap in the "healthy" frozen dessert market: a product that is simultaneously **high-protein, clean-label, and genuinely delicious** — without sacrificing ingredient integrity.

The current landscape is littered with protein ice creams that rely on artificial sweeteners (erythritol, monk fruit extract, sucralose), gums and stabilizers (guar gum, carob gum, carrageenan), sugar alcohols, and ultra-processed protein isolates. These products market themselves as health foods while delivering an ingredient list that belongs in a chemistry lab.

**Humble Bee is different.** Every pint is built from a short, recognizable list of whole-food, regeneratively sourced, and ethically produced ingredients. Sweetened exclusively with raw honey or organic maple syrup. Protein from grass-fed whey. Fat from pasture-raised egg yolks and grass-fed whole milk. No shortcuts. No compromises.

### The Opportunity

- The global protein ice cream market is growing rapidly, driven by health-conscious consumers who refuse to choose between performance nutrition and genuine food.
- Existing "healthy" ice cream brands (Halo Top, Enlightened, Arctic Zero) have captured market share through clever marketing, but none have addressed the clean-label demand at the premium tier.
- A small but growing cohort of consumers — holistic foodies, functional athletes, and ingredient-literate buyers — are actively seeking a product that does not yet exist at scale.

**Humble Bee is built to be that product.**

### The Brand Promise

> *"Fit Freeze. Real Food. No Compromises."*

Every decision — from the farms we source from to the pint in your freezer — reflects a commitment to doing things the right way, even when it's harder and more expensive.

## V. Technical Ingredient Specifications

### Sourcing Standards

#### "Regenerative" Label Requirements

To carry the Humble Bee regenerative sourcing designation, all dairy suppliers must meet the following criteria:

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Grazing** | Year-round pasture access; minimum 120 days on pasture per year |
| **Feed** | No grain supplementation; 100% grass and forage diet |
| **Soil Practices** | Active soil health management (cover crops, rotational grazing, composting) |
| **Antibiotic/Hormone Use** | Zero rBGH/rBST; no routine antibiotic use |
| **Third-Party Certification** | Preferred: Savory Institute, Regenerative Organic Certified (ROC), or equivalent |
| **Animal Welfare** | Pasture density must allow natural grazing behavior; no confinement feeding |

#### "Pasture-Raised" Egg Yolk Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Outdoor Access** | Minimum 108 sq ft per bird (Certified Humane Pasture-Raised standard) |
| **Diet** | Pasture foraging supplemented with non-GMO, soy-free feed |
| **Certification** | Certified Humane Pasture-Raised or equivalent |
| **Yolk Quality** | Deep orange yolk color (indicator of carotenoid-rich pasture diet); tested per batch |

#### Grass-Fed Whey Protein Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Source** | Derived exclusively from grass-fed, pasture-raised cows |
| **Processing** | Cold-processed, micro-filtered (no acid processing) |
| **Additives** | No lecithin, artificial flavors, or flow agents |
| **Protein Concentration** | Minimum 80% protein by weight (whey concentrate preferred over isolate for micronutrient retention) |
| **Certification** | Preferred: Non-GMO Project Verified, third-party heavy metal tested |

#### Raw Honey Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Processing** | Unfiltered, unheated (never exceeds 95°F to preserve enzymes and pollen) |
| **Source** | U.S.-based apiaries preferred; single-origin where feasible |
| **Testing** | Annual third-party testing for pesticide residue and adulteration |

#### Organic Maple Syrup Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Certification** | USDA Certified Organic |
| **Grade** | Grade A, Dark Color / Robust Taste (higher mineral content, deeper flavor) |
| **Additives** | Zero; pure maple syrup only |

### Our Clean Label USP

The Humble Bee differentiator is not just what we put in — it's what we leave out. Our label is our billboard. Every ingredient is:

- **Pronounceable** — No xanthan gum, polysorbate 80, or carrageenan.
- **Traceable** — Every ingredient can be sourced back to a specific farm or apiary.
- **Functional** — Each ingredient serves a nutritional or flavor purpose. No fillers.
- **Honest** — No "natural flavors" hiding an undisclosed blend of chemicals.

This is the standard the market has been asking for. Humble Bee delivers it.

## VIII. Operational Strategy

### Channel 1: Direct-to-Consumer (DTC)

**Platform:** Dedicated Shopify storefront (humblebeeicecream.com)

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Shipping** | Nationwide insulated cold-chain shipping via Dry Ice or Gel Pack system (UPS/FedEx 2-day guaranteed) |
| **Subscription Model** | Monthly "Hive Membership" — 3-pint, 6-pint, or 12-pint recurring options; 10–15% discount; free shipping above threshold |
| **One-Time Purchase** | Full catalog available; minimum order 3 pints for cold-chain economics |
| **Build-a-Box** | Custom flavor selection per shipment |
| **Loyalty Program** | Points per purchase, redeemable for free pints or branded merchandise |

**DTC Priority Rationale:** Direct channel preserves margin, enables direct customer feedback loops, and builds owned audience (email/SMS list) not dependent on retailer relationships.

### Channel 2: Wholesale — Specialty & Natural Retail

**Target Retail Partners (Year 1–2):**

| Retailer | Tier | Target Entry Timeline |
|----------|------|-----------------------|
| Whole Foods Market (regional) | Tier 1 | Year 1 Q3–Q4 |
| Sprouts Farmers Market | Tier 1 | Year 1 Q3–Q4 |
| Natural Grocers | Tier 2 | Year 2 Q1 |
| Earth Fare | Tier 2 | Year 2 Q1–Q2 |
| Local/Regional Health Food Co-ops | Tier 3 | Ongoing |

**Go-to-Market Approach:**
1. Establish proof-of-concept on DTC with strong reviews and reorder rate data.
2. Leverage Coleman's agribusiness network and broker relationships for initial buyer introductions.
3. Apply to Whole Foods Local & Regional program as entry point before national pitch.
4. Use Grayson's ops infrastructure to ensure shelf-ready compliance (PLU codes, EDI, slotting documentation).

### Manufacturing & Co-Packing

- **Phase 1 (Year 1):** Partner with a certified co-packer specializing in frozen desserts with regenerative/organic handling capability.
- **Phase 2 (Year 2–3):** Explore dedicated production facility or kitchen incubator space as volume justifies.
- **Quality Control:** Batch testing protocol at co-packer; random QC pulls per production run; protein content verified via third-party lab.

### Cold-Chain Logistics

- **Warehousing:** Temperature-controlled 3PL partner for DTC fulfillment.
- **Wholesale Distribution:** Region-specific frozen food distributors (UNFI, KeHE) for natural retail placement.
- **Packaging Standard:** Insulated mailers with dry ice for DTC; standard frozen distribution for wholesale.
